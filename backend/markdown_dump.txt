## 1. Algorithm Analysis
- **Ways to Analyze Algorithms:** There are various methods to scrutinize algorithms, each offering unique insights.
- **Worst-Case Analysis:** This approach assumes all inputs are challenging, providing a conservative estimate.
- **Average-Case Analysis:** Here, we consider typical scenarios, often requiring fewer resources.
- **Amortized Analysis:** This method tracks cost changes over time during algorithm execution, as seen in Heap Sort's efficient data organization.

---

## 2. Probabilistic Search Estimation
- **Search Operations:** The expected number of comparisons for a successful search can be calculated using probabilities.
- **Unsuccessful Searches:** In contrast, an unsuccessful search often requires a number of comparisons equal to the dataset size.

---

## 3. Mathematical Decomposition
- **Unique Properties:** The divisibility properties of matrix diagonal entries are linked to their determinants, ensuring a unique decomposition.
- **Pattern Recognition:** These properties aid in identifying patterns within mathematical structures.
